Research scope

In metastatic cancers, tumors spread and evolve as heterogeneous cell populations with distinct molecular programs. This heterogeneity drives mixed treatment responses and creates resistant niches that survive therapy. My work focuses on connecting tumor evolution, transcriptomic states, and clinically actionable response patterns in patient-centered models.

Research interests

  • Molecular tumor heterogeneity and clonal dynamics in metastatic cancers
  • Metastatic development and evolutionary selection under treatment pressure
  • Patient-derived organoid modeling for treatment response and biomarker discovery
